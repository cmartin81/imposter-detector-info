How imposters attack

Multi-layered AI detection

Three independent analysis engines work in parallel to produce a unified threat score — catching even the most sophisticated attacks.

Advanced Image Recognition

Imposters download your admin's profile picture and use it as their own — sometimes with subtle filters, crops, or color shifts to avoid detection.

Our deep perceptual analysis generates a visual fingerprint of every avatar and compares them at the structural level. Compression, resizing, mirroring — none of it fools the engine.

Perceptual hashing Visual fingerprinting

Unicode Deobfuscation

Attackers replace characters with visually identical lookalikes from other alphabets — swapping a Latin "o" for a Cyrillic "о", or an "I" for an "l". Invisible to the human eye.

Our confusable mapping engine normalizes every character across 100K+ unicode code points, stripping away diacritics, invisible characters, and homoglyph substitutions to reveal the true identity.

Homoglyph detection Cyrillic mapping

Behavioral Risk Scoring

Impersonation accounts are almost always brand new — created minutes before the attack. A 2-hour-old account copying your 2-year admin is a dead giveaway.

Tiered temporal analysis scores account age on a sliding scale. Accounts under 24 hours trigger maximum risk, and name similarity from new accounts triggers automatic escalation.

Temporal analysis Risk escalation

Automated Threat Response

Every detected member receives a unified threat score from 0 to 100. Based on that score, the bot takes action automatically — so you don't have to be online 24/7.

High-confidence imposters are auto-kicked instantly. Uncertain cases trigger a notification to your alert channel for manual review. Safe members are silently cleared. You're free to adjust both thresholds to match your server's risk tolerance.
